# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Guild Wars 2 players using Blish HUD during active play. They need to glance at,
check, edit, and reorganize recurring personal activities without leaving the
game for a spreadsheet or separate productivity app. Users may play on compact
monitors, high-DPI displays, or with UI scaling needs.

## Product Purpose

Taskmaster is a local, user-defined recurring checklist for Guild Wars 2. It
organizes tasks into freeform tabs and supports schedules, counters, subtasks,
notes, clipboard content, and sharing. Success means routine management is fast,
reliable, readable, and unobtrusive while the game remains the primary focus.

## Brand Personality

Practical, dependable, and quietly polished. The interface should feel native to
Blish HUD and purposeful rather than decorative.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic web-dashboard styling, oversized decorative surfaces, excessive
animation, dense controls with tiny click targets, and visual treatments that
compete with the game. Do not hide core actions behind hover-only interactions
or require external accounts for normal use.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the game primary: information should be glanceable and the window should
   remain compact.
2. Make frequent actions direct: checking, copying, editing, selecting, and
   reordering should require minimal pointer travel.
3. Preserve user context: edits, expansion, sorting, and refreshes must not cause
   unexpected scrolling or selection changes.
4. Scale density, not just pixels: larger text and targets must remain aligned,
   readable, and usable on small displays.
5. Keep behavior predictable: standard clicks, context menus, modifiers, and
   drag interactions should follow familiar desktop conventions.
6. Keep curated content opt-in: built-in presets should materialize as normal
   local checklist groups inside user-chosen tabs, not as separate tracker
   screens or network-dependent services.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support configurable text and control sizing, strong contrast, visible hover and
selected states, sufficiently large interaction targets, and color-independent
state cues. The minimum window size and layout must adapt to scaling without
clipping core controls.
